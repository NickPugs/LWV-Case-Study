The League of Austin Women Voters Austin Area

Website Redesign

Research

Surveys

We surveyed 74 people to understand their political interests &awareness of the League. We found that people want a trustworthy, unbiased source of information when making voting decisions. We also found that most people were interrested in volunteering their time but didn't know the proper channels to do that.

Stakeholder Interviews

Over three interviews with members of the League, a 1:1 with the current president, and two group interviews with additional members, some of our main takeaways were their strong desire for a more modern, streamlined website; wanting to attract a more diverse membership base; and that the Voterâ€™s Guide is the main traffic driver.

Testing

We conducted 8 usability tests on the initial site and found it to be lacking clear navigation and appealing design. After several iterations, we conducted 15 tests on our mid and hi-fidelity designs with the same users. They were delighted by the design changes and ease of navigation.


Problem Statement

We are redesigning the League of Women Voter's website to allow easier access to valuable unbiased facts about political elections. We believe this will increase the dissemination of important information that ultimately leads to more educated voters.

How might we improve the LWV-AA website so that concerned citizens get unbiased information they are looking for quickly so they can vote with confidence?
